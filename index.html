Shivoham Lab is an independent research lab founded by Shouraya Sharma. The lab explores practical AI, systems engineering, and developer tools with an open-source default. Research papers are a goal but the primary focus is shipping real, useful projects. The lab is actively looking for collaborators people who bring fresh ideas and want to execute together.

About

What Shivoham Lab is, and why it exists.

The lab is designed to move fast without becoming generic: a place for focused research, concrete systems, and open tools that solve real problems.

Independent by design

Shivoham Lab is not a corporate R&D arm. It is a small, founder-led lab built to explore ideas that can be shipped, tested, and shared in public.

Meaning of Shivoham

Shivoham (शिवोहम्) is Sanskrit for “I am Shiv.” Here it signals a disciplined builder identity: clear intent, calm execution, and useful output.

Founder profile

Founded by Shouraya, a CS undergrad at MIT Bengaluru (2024–2028). At the lab, Shouraya works as Founder & Builder, shaping projects from concept to release.

Mission

Build open-source tools, research software, and infrastructure that make AI and systems work in the real world. The emphasis is on practical value, clean engineering, and long-lived code.

Open source Build in public Practical AI Systems thinking

Working style

The lab prefers small, high-leverage products with well-documented interfaces, thoughtful defaults, and a strong bias toward solving problems users actually have.
